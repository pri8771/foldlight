## What Is Foldlight?

Foldlight is a beautiful, relaxing puzzle game for iPhone. Instead of matching colored blocks or sorting colored tubes like every other puzzle game, Foldlight gives you a magical board that you can **fold** — like a piece of paper — to solve the puzzle.

When you fold parts of the board together, magical things happen: a beam of light bends, a hidden path appears, a bridge grows, a door unlocks. Every puzzle is a moment of discovery.

---

## Who Is It For?

**Primary Audience:** Adults 22–45 who play casual mobile games
- People who enjoyed Monument Valley, Alto's Odyssey, or The Room
- Commuters, people who play before bed, or during short breaks
- Players who want something beautiful and satisfying, not stressful

**Secondary Audience:** Puzzle enthusiasts (any age) who want a fresh mechanic

---

## What Makes It Special?

**1. Completely Original Mechanic**
There is no other puzzle game that lets you fold the board itself. Every other major puzzle game — match-3, color sort, screw puzzles, merge puzzles — works by moving pieces. Foldlight is about changing the shape of the puzzle.

**2. Infinite Levels, Always Fresh**
The game generates new puzzles algorithmically, so you never run out of content. Each day brings a new Daily Puzzle. The infinite mode generates puzzles forever.

**3. A World Worth Coming Back To**
Every puzzle you solve restores a magical broken world. Broken islands are rebuilt. Constellations light up again. Tiny creatures return. There is always something to work toward beyond "next level."

**4. Designed to Feel Premium**
Foldlight is designed to feel like a game made by a major studio — beautiful animations, satisfying sound design, haptic feedback, a cohesive visual world. It does not feel like a free clicker game. It feels like a product worth paying for.

## How Does the Game Work? (Simple Version)

1. You see a grid of magical tiles — some lit, some dark, some with symbols
2. You draw a fold line across the board
3. One side of the board flips over onto the other — like folding paper
4. When tiles land on top of each other, they combine: a light tile + a mirror tile = the beam changes direction
5. If the light beam reaches the crystal, you win
6. You earn Light Fragments, which restore pieces of a beautiful broken world

That's the core of the game. As you progress, new tile combinations are introduced, boards get larger, and folds become more creative.

---

## What Does Progress Look Like?

Players always have something to work toward:

- **Stars** — earn 1–3 stars per puzzle based on how efficiently you solved it
- **Light Fragments** — currency earned by solving puzzles
- **World Restoration** — spend fragments to rebuild broken islands, light constellations, collect creatures
- **Biomes** — 10 visual themes that unlock as you progress (Crystal Cave → Glass Forest → Star Map → etc.)
- **Daily Puzzles** — a new hand-quality puzzle every day, with streaks rewarded
- **Infinite Mode** — endless generated puzzles for any player level

## How Does the Game Make Money?

Foldlight makes money through optional purchases. Players never need to pay to finish the game.

**What we sell:**
- **Board Skins** ($1.99 each) — different visual themes for the puzzle board
- **Hint Packs** ($0.99–$3.99) — hints that show the next best fold
- **No Ads** ($2.99) — remove optional reward ads permanently
- **Starter Bundle** ($4.99) — No Ads + 30 Hints + a premium skin
- **Challenge Pass** ($2.99/month) — exclusive challenge puzzles and early biome access

**What we don't do:**
- No energy systems
- No forcing purchases to continue
- No pay-to-win mechanics
- No gambling mechanics

The goal is to make players *want* to buy cosmetics because the game is beautiful, not because they're stuck.

## Competitive Landscape

| Game | What It Does | Foldlight Advantage |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| Monument Valley | Beautiful puzzle art | More replayable (procedural infinite content) |
| The Room | Intricate mechanical puzzles | More accessible (one-handed, casual sessions) |
| Threes! | Number merging | More visual and narrative |
| Block Blast | Block clearing | More original mechanic, more beautiful |
| Water Sort | Color sorting | Completely different — fold vs pour |

Foldlight has no direct competitor using a fold mechanic. This is a genuine white space.

## Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| Mechanic is confusing for players | Medium | Strong tutorial, one-thumb gesture, gradual introduction |
| Procedural levels feel samey | Medium | 10 biomes, daily puzzles, difficulty tiers, handcrafted onboarding |
| App Store discoverability | High | ASO optimization, gameplay video, press outreach |
| Monetization underperforms | Medium | Multiple revenue streams, iterative pricing tests |
| Development takes too long | Low | Scoped MVP, modular architecture |
